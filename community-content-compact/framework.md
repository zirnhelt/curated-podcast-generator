# Community Content Compact — Scoring Framework

*Evaluating content produced about, from, or on behalf of a community*

---

## About This Framework

This framework evaluates the relationship between **content producers** and the **communities they represent, draw from, or claim to serve** — regardless of how the content was made.

It applies to AI-generated, algorithmically curated, documentary, journalism, academic, NGO-produced, platform-driven, and hybrid content. Two tracks reflect the two fundamentally different relationships a producer can have with a community.

---

## Track Selection

| | Track A — Community-Insider | Track B — External Producer |
|---|---|---|
| **Who** | Community member, non-commercial, self-funded or micro-funded, producing primarily for the community | Institutional, commercial, externally funded, or standing outside the community |
| **Primary risk** | **Capture** — one voice claiming to speak for all without a mandate | **Extraction** — taking stories, credibility, and audience without giving back |
| **Points possible** | **20** | **35** |
| **Verdict scale** | Needs Work / Developing / Sound / Exemplary | Extractive / High Risk / Conditional / Defensible / Community-Serving |

When in doubt, use Track B.

## Track A — Community-Insider Scoring

*6 categories · 20 points possible*

---

### I. Transparency *(Track A max: 4)*

**T1 — Producer Disclosure** *(0–2)*

- 0 · Producer identity not disclosed or obscured
- 1 · Producer named but community connection not explained
- 2 · Producer identity and community connection clearly disclosed

**T2 — Process Disclosure** *(0–2)*

- 0 · No information about how the content was produced
- 1 · General description available on request
- 2 · Full methodology or production process publicly documented

### II. Source Integrity *(Track A max: 3)*

**S1 — Source Documentation** *(0–2)*

- 0 · Sources not documented; no record of engagement
- 1 · Sources credited but nature of engagement not documented
- 2 · Sources documented with evidence of appropriate, respectful engagement

**S2 — Credit** *(0–1)*

- 0 · No credit to sources

### IV. Consent & Attribution *(Track A max: 4)*

> **N/A guidance:** N/A applies only when no living people or real named entities are identified, quoted, or represented in the content. Content featuring real organizations (e.g. in PSAs, partner mentions, or community spotlights) does **not** qualify for N/A — those organizations are real entities being represented. N/A must be documented with a clear rationale.

**C1 — Consent Mechanism** *(0–2, or N/A)*

- 0 · No consent process exists
- 1 · Consent sought from some participants or featured entities; others included without documented process

**C2 — Living Person Protection** *(0–2, or N/A)*

- 0 · No formal policy on attributed voice or representation
- 1 · Informal norms or editorial guidelines only
- 2 · Formal, documented policy; actively enforced; community members can invoke it
- N/A · No living people or real named entities are identified, quoted, or represented *(document below)*

### V. Benefit Flow *(Track A max: 5)*

**B1 — Output Ownership** *(0–2)*

- 0 · Individual producer holds all rights with no community claim

**B2 — Audience Relationship Ownership** *(0–2)*

- 0 · External platform controls the audience relationship entirely
- 1 · Partially community-held — some direct relationship exists alongside platform distribution
- 2 · Community or producer holds a direct subscriber or contact relationship independent of third-party platforms

**B3 — Archive** *(0–1)*

- 0 · Archive controlled by an external platform with no community access
- 1 · Archive under producer or community control

### Track A — Verdict

| Score | Verdict | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| *Any DQ* | **Disqualified** | Fails regardless of score. |
| 0–5 | **Needs Work** | Foundational accountability conditions are missing. Address before continuing. |
| 6–11 | **Developing** | Some conditions met. Identify the lowest-scoring categories and address them. |
| 12–16 | **Sound** | Core conditions are met. Named gaps are worth addressing over time. |
| 17–20 | **Exemplary** | Strong accountability conditions in place. Document and share this model. |

### Track A — Continuous Improvement

Track A producers are typically operating at small scale with limited resources. The improvement path is sequenced by effort, not urgency.

**Transparency:** Disclose your community connection explicitly (T1 → 2). Document your process publicly (T2 → 2).

**Source Integrity:** Document the nature of your source engagement beyond credit (S1 → 2).

**Consent:** Establish a consistent notification or consent process for all organizations and individuals featured (C1 → 2). Document a living person protection policy (C2 → 2).

**Benefit Flow:** Establish a direct subscriber relationship alongside platform distribution (B2 → 2).

**Accountability:** Publish a corrections policy with a documented response process (A1 → 2).

**Reaching Track B standards:** When a Track A project grows to include commercial revenue, external funding, or institutional production, re-evaluate against Track B.

## Track B — External Producer Scoring

*7 categories · 35 points possible*

---

### I. Transparency *(Track B max: 7)*

**T1 — Producer Disclosure** *(0–3)*

- 0 · Producer identity not disclosed or obscured
- 1 · Producer named but relationship to community not explained
- 2 · Producer identity, role, and relationship to community clearly disclosed
- 3 · Producer holds accreditation directly relevant to this content's subject matter from a recognized authoritative institution or government body — and that relevance is disclosed alongside the credential

**T2 — Process Disclosure** *(0–2)*

- 0 · No information about how the content was produced
- 1 · General description available on request
- 2 · Full methodology or production process publicly documented

**T3 — Funding & Motivation Disclosure** *(0–2)*

- 0 · Not disclosed
- 1 · Funder named but interests unspecified
- 2 · Full disclosure including commercial interests, editorial influence, and conflicts

**Section score: ___ / 7**

---

### II. Source Integrity *(Track B max: 4)*

**S1 — Source Documentation** *(0–2)*

- 0 · Sources not documented; no record of engagement
- 1 · Sources credited but nature of engagement not documented
- 2 · Sources documented with evidence of appropriate, respectful engagement

**S2 — Compensation & Credit** *(0–2)*

- 0 · No compensation or meaningful credit to source communities or individuals
- 1 · Credit provided but no material compensation
- 2 · Appropriate compensation and credit; community shares meaningfully in any benefit

**Section score: ___ / 4**

---

### III. Displacement *(Track B max: 5)*

*Does this content compete with, substitute for, or undermine creators already doing this work in the community?*

**D1 — Existing Creator Assessment** *(0–3)*

- 0 · No assessment conducted
- 1 · Assessment conducted — potential overlap identified but unresolved
- 2 · Confirmed preexisting void: this content addresses a gap where no community creator currently exists
- 3 · Active, documented collaboration with existing community creators who retain meaningful creative authority

**D2 — Investment in Community Creator Capacity** *(0–2)*

- 0 · No investment or consideration
- 1 · Acknowledged as future consideration
- 2 · Active, funded investment in local creator capacity that outlasts this content

**Section score: ___ / 5**

### IV. Consent & Attribution *(Track B max: 4)*

> **N/A guidance:** N/A applies only when no living people or real named entities are identified, quoted, or represented. N/A is treated as 2 points assumed but must be documented.

**C1 — Consent Mechanism** *(0–2, or N/A)*

- 0 · No consent process exists
- 1 · Consent sought from some participants; others included without consent
- 2 · Explicit, informed, revocable consent required from all participants and sources
- N/A · No living people or real named entities are identified, quoted, or represented *(document below)*

**C2 — Living Person Protection** *(0–2, or N/A)*

- 0 · No formal policy on attributed voice or representation
- 1 · Informal norms or editorial guidelines only
- 2 · Formal, documented policy; actively enforced; community members can invoke it
- N/A · No living people or real named entities are identified, quoted, or represented *(document below)*

**Section score: ___ / 4**

### V. Benefit Flow *(Track B max: 6)*

**B1 — Output Ownership** *(0–2)*

- 0 · External producer or platform
- 1 · Shared or contractually ambiguous
- 2 · Community holds clear, documented ownership

**B2 — Audience Relationship Ownership** *(0–2)*

- 0 · External platform or producer controls the audience relationship
- 1 · Shared or partially community-held
- 2 · Community owns the direct subscriber, contact, or distribution relationship

**B3 — Archive Ownership** *(0–2)*

- 0 · External producer or platform controls the archive
- 1 · Shared or hosted with external dependency
- 2 · Community holds and controls the archive independently

### VI. Accountability *(Track B max: 4)*

**A1 — Feedback Mechanism** *(0–2)*

- 0 · None
- 1 · Exists but inaccessible without technical literacy or platform access
- 2 · Accessible to all community members; corrections have demonstrated, documented effect

**A2 — Editorial Authority** *(0–2)*

- 0 · External producer holds final editorial authority
- 1 · Shared — community can advise but not override
- 2 · Community holds final editorial authority with an enforcement mechanism

**Section score: ___ / 4**

---

### VII. Governance Durability *(Track B max: 5)*

*Will the structures that protect the community survive the project?*

**G1 — Funding Model** *(0–3)*

- 0 · Project-based or pilot only — no commitment beyond this content
- 1 · Mixed — some operational continuity, some project-based
- 2 · Multi-year operational funding committed from an external funder
- 3 · Independently funded or resourced — community-owned or self-sustaining model

**G2 — Governance Architecture** *(0–2)*

- 0 · None — relies on goodwill and informal relationships
- 1 · Informal documentation or editorial guidelines
- 2 · Formal, documented, community-ratified governance framework with review cycle

### Track B — Verdict

| Score | Verdict | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| *Any DQ* | **Disqualified** | Fails regardless of score. Do not proceed. |
| 0–10 | **Extractive** | This content takes from the community more than it serves it. Do not proceed without fundamental redesign. |
| 11–18 | **High Risk** | Significant gaps. Specific conditions must be met before publication or deployment. |
| 19–25 | **Conditional** | Material gaps remain. Requires a written remediation plan before proceeding. |
| 26–31 | **Defensible** | Core conditions are met. Proceed with active accountability maintenance. Revisit annually. |
| 32–35 | **Community-Serving** | Strong structural conditions in place. Document this model. |
