# Community Content Compact — Scoring Framework

**The Community Content Compact**

*Evaluating content produced about, from, or on behalf of a community*
*2 disqualifiers · 7 categories · 35 points possible*

---

## About This Framework

This framework evaluates the relationship between **content producers** and the **communities they represent, draw from, or claim to serve** — regardless of how the content was made.

It applies to any externally produced content: AI-generated, algorithmically curated, documentary, journalism, academic, NGO-produced, platform-driven, or hybrid. It also applies to community-insider production, with adjusted criteria that reflect the different risk profile of insider production (see Track A vs. Track B below).

---

## Track Selection

Choose a track before scoring. The disqualifiers and section structure are the same for both tracks. Individual question criteria differ where noted.

| Track | For whom | Primary risk |
|---|---|---|
| **Track A** | Producer is a member of, directly employed by, or directly accountable to the community they cover | **Capture** — one voice claiming to speak for the whole without a clear mandate |
| **Track B** | Producer stands outside the community they cover | **Extraction** — taking stories, credibility, revenue, and audience from the community without giving back |

When in doubt, use Track B. If you are uncertain whether you qualify as a community insider, you probably do not.

## Scored Categories

Score each question using the scale provided per question. Not all questions share the same maximum. Where Track A and Track B criteria differ, both are shown. Total all categories for a final score out of 35.

---

### I. Transparency

*Can any audience member understand what this content is, who made it, and why?*

**T1 — Producer Disclosure** *(0–3)*

**Track A and Track B share this scale.**

- 0 · Producer identity not disclosed or obscured
- 1 · Producer named but relationship to community not explained
- 2 · Producer identity, role, and relationship to community clearly disclosed
- 3 · Producer holds accreditation *directly relevant to this content's subject matter* from a recognized authoritative institution or government body *(e.g. professional designation, degree, certification, licensing body)* — and that relevance is disclosed alongside the credential

*Track A note: "relationship to community" includes disclosing the basis of your insider status. Being a community member is not self-evident to all audiences. State it explicitly.*

**T2 — Process Disclosure** *(0–2)*

- 0 · No information about how the content was produced
- 1 · General description available on request
- 2 · Full methodology or production process publicly documented

**T3 — Funding & Motivation Disclosure** *(0–2)*

- 0 · Not disclosed
- 1 · Funder named but interests unspecified
- 2 · Full disclosure including commercial interests, editorial influence, and conflicts

### II. Source Integrity

*Where did the stories, voices, images, data, and cultural material in this content come from?*

**S1 — Source Documentation** *(0–2)*

- 0 · Sources not documented; no record of engagement
- 1 · Sources credited but nature of engagement not documented
- 2 · Sources documented with evidence of appropriate, respectful engagement

**S2 — Compensation & Credit** *(0–2)*

**Track B:**
- 0 · No compensation or meaningful credit to source communities or individuals
- 1 · Credit provided but no material compensation
- 2 · Appropriate compensation and credit; community shares meaningfully in any benefit

**Track A:**
- 0 · No credit to individuals whose stories or knowledge informed the content
- 1 · Named credit to individuals; collective benefit not considered
- 2 · Named credit and a documented approach to ensuring collective benefit — not just the producer's — from the content

*Track A note: community-insider producers face a specific risk of converting shared community stories into individual platform value. S2 asks whether the benefit flows to individuals and to the collective, not just to the producer.*

**Section score: ___ / 4**

### III. Displacement / Mandate

*Track B: Does this content compete with, substitute for, or undermine creators already doing this work in the community?*
*Track A: Do you have a genuine community mandate to produce this content, and are you building collective capacity or individual platform?*

---

**D1 — Track B: Existing Creator Assessment** *(0–3)*

- 0 · No assessment conducted
- 1 · Assessment conducted — potential overlap identified but unresolved
- 2 · Confirmed preexisting void: this content addresses a gap where no community creator currently exists
- 3 · Active, documented collaboration with existing community creators who retain meaningful creative authority

**D1 — Track A: Mandate Assessment** *(0–3)*

- 0 · No documented mandate; producer is self-appointed
- 1 · Informal community recognition; no explicit mandate process
- 2 · Explicit mandate: community members have affirmed this producer's role through a documented process *(election, appointment, editorial agreement, or equivalent)*
- 3 · Active, documented accountability to the community for editorial decisions, with a clear mechanism to rescind the mandate

---

**D2 — Track B: Investment in Community Creator Capacity** *(0–2)*

- 0 · No investment or consideration
- 1 · Acknowledged as future consideration
- 2 · Active, funded investment in local creator capacity that outlasts this content

**D2 — Track A: Building Collective vs. Individual Capacity** *(0–2)*

- 0 · Content primarily builds the producer's individual platform or reputation within the community
- 1 · Some structural support for other community voices; not yet primary
- 2 · Content is designed and documented to build collective capacity — other community members can continue it, adapt it, or supersede it without the original producer

### IV. Consent & Attribution

*Who was asked, on what terms, and who can say no?*

> **N/A guidance:** If no living people are identified, quoted, represented, or drawn upon in this content, C1 and C2 may be scored N/A. N/A is treated as 2 points but must be documented in the Notes section with a clear rationale.

**C1 — Consent Mechanism** *(0–2, or N/A)*

**Track A and Track B share this scale.**

- 0 · No consent process exists
- 1 · Consent sought from some participants; others included without consent
- 2 · Explicit, informed, revocable consent required from all participants and sources
- N/A · No living people are identified, quoted, or represented in this content *(document below)*

**C2 — Living Person Protection** *(0–2, or N/A)*

**Track A and Track B share this scale.**

- 0 · No formal policy on attributed voice or representation
- 1 · Informal norms or editorial guidelines only
- 2 · Formal, documented policy; actively enforced; community members can invoke it
- N/A · No living people are identified, quoted, or represented in this content *(document below)*

*Track A note: insider producers are often trusted by default, which makes informal norms more dangerous. An insider who speaks for community members without a formal consent mechanism does so with perceived legitimacy that makes the harm harder to recognize and challenge.*

### V. Benefit Flow

*Who owns what this content creates?*

**B1 — Output Ownership** *(0–2)*

**Track B:**
- 0 · External producer or platform
- 1 · Shared or contractually ambiguous
- 2 · Community holds clear, documented ownership

**Track A:**
- 0 · Individual producer holds ownership with no collective claim
- 1 · Shared or contractually ambiguous between producer and community
- 2 · Community holds clear, documented ownership — including what happens to the content if the producer leaves

**B2 — Audience Relationship Ownership** *(0–2)*

**Track A and Track B share this scale.**

- 0 · External platform or producer controls the audience relationship
- 1 · Shared or partially community-held
- 2 · Community owns the direct subscriber, contact, or distribution relationship

**B3 — Archive Ownership** *(0–2)*

**Track A and Track B share this scale.**

- 0 · External producer or platform controls the archive
- 1 · Shared or hosted with external dependency
- 2 · Community holds and controls the archive independently

**Section score: ___ / 6**

### VI. Accountability

*Can the community correct, challenge, or remove content — and does that power mean anything?*

**A1 — Feedback Mechanism** *(0–2)*

**Track A and Track B share this scale.**

- 0 · None
- 1 · Exists but inaccessible without technical literacy or platform access
- 2 · Accessible to all community members; corrections have demonstrated, documented effect

**A2 — Editorial Authority** *(0–2)*

**Track B:**
- 0 · External producer holds final editorial authority
- 1 · Shared — community can advise but not override
- 2 · Community holds final editorial authority with an enforcement mechanism

**Track A:**
- 0 · Individual insider producer holds final authority with no accountability to the community
- 1 · Informal community advisory input; no override mechanism
- 2 · Documented accountability structure: the community can challenge, correct, or remove content through a formal process that does not require the producer's cooperation

*Track A note: insider editorial authority is desirable in principle but can become a chokepoint. The question is not whether the insider has authority, but whether the community can override it when the insider is wrong or captured.*

**Section score: ___ / 4**

---

### VII. Governance Durability

*Will the structures that protect the community survive the project?*

**G1 — Funding Model** *(0–3)*

**Track A and Track B share this scale.**

- 0 · Project-based or pilot only — no commitment beyond this content
- 1 · Mixed — some operational continuity, some project-based
- 2 · Multi-year operational funding committed from an external funder
- 3 · Independently funded or resourced — community-owned or self-sustaining model with no external funding dependency

**G2 — Governance Architecture** *(0–2)*

**Track B:**
- 0 · None — relies on goodwill and informal relationships
- 1 · Informal documentation or editorial guidelines
- 2 · Formal, documented, community-ratified governance framework with review cycle

**Track A:**
- 0 · No succession plan or governance document; content ends if the producer leaves
- 1 · Informal understanding of how the content would continue; not documented
- 2 · Formal, documented succession and governance framework, community-ratified, with a review cycle — the content's future does not depend on any single person

*Track A note: the single greatest governance risk in community-insider production is that the producer is the institution. Governance durability asks: what survives you?*

## Verdict

| Score | Verdict | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| *Any DQ* | **Disqualified** | Fails regardless of score. Do not proceed. |
| 0–10 | **Extractive** | This content takes from the community more than it serves it. Do not proceed without fundamental redesign. |
| 11–18 | **High Risk** | Significant gaps in transparency, ownership, or accountability. Specific conditions must be met before publication or deployment. |
| 19–25 | **Conditional** | Some conditions met, but material gaps remain. Requires a written remediation plan addressing the lowest-scoring dimensions before proceeding. |
| 26–31 | **Defensible** | Core conditions are met. Proceed, with active maintenance of accountability mechanisms. Revisit governance annually. |
| 32–35 | **Community-Serving** | Strong structural conditions in place. Document this model — it is worth sharing and replicating. |
