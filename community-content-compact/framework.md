# Creative Content Accountability Framework

**The Community Content Compact**

*Evaluating content produced about, from, or on behalf of a community*

-----

## About This Framework

This framework evaluates the relationship between **content producers** and the **communities they represent, draw from, or claim to serve** — regardless of how the content was made.

It applies to AI-generated, algorithmically curated, documentary, journalism, academic, NGO-produced, platform-driven, hobbyist, or hybrid content. The framework routes to one of two tracks based on the producer's relationship to the community. The disqualifiers apply to both.

-----

## Step 1 — Select a Track

Answer all four questions. **Track A applies only if all four are Yes.** If any answer is No, use Track B.

|Qualifying Question                                                                                                             |Yes / No|
|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------|
|The producer is a member of the community being represented, or has lived and worked within it in a sustained and meaningful way|        |
|There is no commercial revenue model — no advertising, licensing fees, or subscription income                                   |        |
|The content is self-funded, volunteer-funded, or micro-funded with no institutional grants or external funding dependencies     |        |
|The primary intended audience is the community itself or directly adjacent communities                                          |        |

**→ All Yes: use Track A** *(community-insider, non-commercial, small-scale)*
**→ Any No: use Track B** *(external, institutional, commercial, or mixed)*

-----

## Step 2 — Automatic Disqualifiers

Answer these before scoring. Any **Yes** ends the evaluation regardless of track — no score applies.

|#  |Question                                                                                                                  |Yes / No|
|---|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|--------|
|DQ1|The content misrepresents its authorship, origin, or producer identity                                                    |        |
|DQ2|The content attributes views, statements, or cultural knowledge to living people without their explicit, revocable consent|        |

# TRACK A

## Community-Insider Production

*Non-commercial · Self-funded · Community-facing*
*6 categories · 20 points possible*

### A — I. Transparency

*Is it clear what this content is and who made it?*

**A-T1 — Producer Disclosure** *(0–2)*

- 0 · Producer identity not disclosed
- 1 · Producer named but relationship to community not explained
- 2 · Producer identity and community relationship clearly disclosed

**A-T2 — Process Disclosure** *(0–2)*

- 0 · No information about how the content was produced
- 1 · Basic description available
- 2 · Full process or methodology documented and accessible

*Where did the material come from, and is that clear?*

**A-S1 — Source Documentation** *(0–2)*

- 0 · Sources not documented or credited

- 2 · Sources documented with evidence of appropriate, respectful engagement

**A-S2 — Credit** *(0–1)*

- 1 · Clear, appropriate credit given

*Note: Compensation is not expected at this scale and is not scored.*

**A-D1 — Existing Creator Assessment** *(0–2)*

- 0 · No consideration of existing creators
- 1 · Potential overlap considered but unresolved
- 2 · Content fills a confirmed void, or clearly complements rather than competes with existing community creators

## Track A — Verdict

|Score   |Verdict         |Meaning                                                                                        |
|--------|----------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|*Any DQ*|**Disqualified**|Fails regardless of score. Do not proceed.                                                     |
|0–5     |**Needs Work**  |Core accountability conditions are not met. Address transparency and consent before continuing.|
|6–11    |**Developing**  |Some conditions met. Identifiable gaps worth addressing, but not a fundamental problem.        |
|12–16   |**Sound**       |This is responsible community production. Named gaps are worth improving over time.            |
|17–20   |**Exemplary**   |Strong practice across all conditions relevant to this scale of production.                    |

## Track A — Continuous Improvement

A Sound or Exemplary score means the core accountability conditions are met. The practices below are not scored — they are a growth path for producers who want to build toward Track B standards over time, organized by effort.

**Low effort — worth doing regardless of scale**

- Publish a brief "How we're made" statement (funding, tools, producer identity, community connection)
- Document how sources and PSA/partner organizations were selected and whether they were notified
- Establish a public corrections policy: how to submit one, how it will be handled, commitment to note corrections in subsequent episodes
- Include a "Sources we drew from" credit in each episode or publication

**Medium effort — meaningful growth**

- Inventory existing creators in your space: local journalists, Indigenous media, community radio, other podcasters. Document whether you fill a gap or risk overlap. Reach out where overlap exists.
- Add a direct subscription option (email list, RSS) alongside any platform distribution so the audience relationship isn't entirely platform-dependent
- Mirror your archive to a location you control independently of any hosting platform

**Aspirational — Track B territory**

- Form an informal community advisory group (3–5 people) who review themes and can flag issues; document their scope and cadence
- Explore co-production with existing local creators where they retain meaningful creative authority over their contribution
- Develop a written governance framework, even a simple one, ratified by community members

# TRACK B

## External Production

*Institutional · Commercial · Mixed · Or any Track A qualifier not met*
*7 categories · 35 points possible*

Score each question using the scale provided. Not all questions share the same maximum. Total all categories for a final score out of 35.

-----

### B — I. Transparency

*Can any audience member understand what this content is, who made it, and why?*

**B-T1 — Producer Disclosure** *(0–3)*

- 0 · Producer identity not disclosed or obscured
- 1 · Producer named but relationship to community not explained
- 2 · Producer identity, role, and relationship to community clearly disclosed
- 3 · Producer holds accreditation *directly relevant to this content's subject matter* from a recognized authoritative institution or government body — and that relevance is disclosed alongside the credential

**B-T2 — Process Disclosure** *(0–2)*

- 0 · No information about how the content was produced
- 1 · General description available on request
- 2 · Full methodology or production process publicly documented

**B-T3 — Funding & Motivation Disclosure** *(0–2)*

- 0 · Not disclosed
- 1 · Funder named but interests unspecified
- 2 · Full disclosure including commercial interests, editorial influence, and conflicts

**Section score: ___ / 7**

-----

### B — II. Source Integrity

*Where did the stories, voices, images, data, and cultural material in this content come from?*

**B-S1 — Source Documentation** *(0–2)*

- 0 · Sources not documented; no record of engagement
- 1 · Sources credited but nature of engagement not documented
- 2 · Sources documented with evidence of appropriate, respectful engagement

**B-S2 — Compensation & Credit** *(0–2)*

- 0 · No compensation or meaningful credit to source communities or individuals
- 1 · Credit provided but no material compensation
- 2 · Appropriate compensation and credit; community shares meaningfully in any benefit

**Section score: ___ / 4**

-----

### B — III. Displacement

*Does this content compete with, substitute for, or undermine creators already doing this work in the community?*

**B-D1 — Existing Creator Assessment** *(0–3)*

- 0 · No assessment conducted
- 1 · Assessment conducted — potential overlap identified but unresolved
- 2 · Confirmed preexisting void: this content addresses a gap where no community creator currently exists
- 3 · Active, documented collaboration with existing community creators who retain meaningful creative authority

**B-D2 — Investment in Community Creator Capacity** *(0–2)*

- 0 · No investment or consideration
- 1 · Acknowledged as future consideration
- 2 · Active, funded investment in local creator capacity that outlasts this content

**Section score: ___ / 5**

-----

### B — IV. Consent & Attribution

*Who was asked, on what terms, and who can say no?*

> **N/A guidance:** If no living people are identified, quoted, represented, or drawn upon, C1 and C2 may be scored N/A. N/A is treated as 2 points but must be documented in the Notes section.

**B-C1 — Consent Mechanism** *(0–2, or N/A)*

- 0 · No consent process exists
- 1 · Consent sought from some participants; others included without consent
- 2 · Explicit, informed, revocable consent required from all participants and sources
- N/A · No living people are identified, quoted, or represented *(document below)*

**B-C2 — Living Person Protection** *(0–2, or N/A)*

- 0 · No formal policy on attributed voice or representation
- 1 · Informal norms or editorial guidelines only
- 2 · Formal, documented policy; actively enforced; community members can invoke it
- N/A · No living people are identified, quoted, or represented *(document below)*

**Section score: ___ / 4**

### B — V. Benefit Flow

*Who owns what this content creates?*

**B-B1 — Output Ownership** *(0–2)*

- 0 · External producer or platform
- 1 · Shared or contractually ambiguous
- 2 · Community holds clear, documented ownership

**B-B2 — Audience Relationship Ownership** *(0–2)*

- 0 · External platform or producer controls the audience relationship
- 1 · Shared or partially community-held
- 2 · Community owns the direct subscriber, contact, or distribution relationship

**B-B3 — Archive Ownership** *(0–2)*

- 0 · External producer or platform controls the archive
- 1 · Shared or hosted with external dependency
- 2 · Community holds and controls the archive independently

### B — VI. Accountability

*Can the community correct, challenge, or remove content — and does that power mean anything?*

**B-A1 — Feedback Mechanism** *(0–2)*

- 0 · None
- 1 · Exists but inaccessible without technical literacy or platform access
- 2 · Accessible to all community members; corrections have demonstrated, documented effect

**B-A2 — Editorial Authority** *(0–2)*

- 0 · External producer holds final editorial authority
- 1 · Shared — community can advise but not override
- 2 · Community holds final editorial authority with an enforcement mechanism

**Section score: ___ / 4**

### B — VII. Governance Durability

*Will the structures that protect the community survive the project?*

**B-G1 — Funding Model** *(0–3)*

- 0 · Project-based or pilot only — no commitment beyond this content
- 1 · Mixed — some operational continuity, some project-based
- 2 · Multi-year operational funding committed from an external funder
- 3 · Independently funded or resourced — community-owned or self-sustaining model with no external funding dependency

**B-G2 — Governance Architecture** *(0–2)*

- 0 · None — relies on goodwill and informal relationships
- 1 · Informal documentation or editorial guidelines
- 2 · Formal, documented, community-ratified governance framework with review cycle

## Track B — Verdict

|Score   |Verdict              |Meaning                                                                                                                                       |
|--------|---------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|*Any DQ*|**Disqualified**     |Fails regardless of score. Do not proceed.                                                                                                    |
|0–10    |**Extractive**       |This content takes from the community more than it serves it. Do not proceed without fundamental redesign.                                    |
|11–18   |**High Risk**        |Significant gaps in transparency, ownership, or accountability. Specific conditions must be met before publication or deployment.             |
|19–25   |**Conditional**      |Some conditions met, but material gaps remain. Requires a written remediation plan addressing the lowest-scoring dimensions before proceeding.|
|26–31   |**Defensible**       |Core conditions are met. Proceed, with active maintenance of accountability mechanisms. Revisit governance annually.                          |
|32–35   |**Community-Serving**|Strong structural conditions in place. Document this model — it is worth sharing and replicating.                                             |
