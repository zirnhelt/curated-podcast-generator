# Episode Harvest: Cariboo Connections - Cariboo Local Affairs
*Saturday, May 30, 2026 · Cariboo Local Affairs*

## Episode Summary
Municipal enforcement tools alone cannot resolve prolific offending in small cities like Williams Lake because the levers that matter — criminal justice, treatment mandates, service funding — sit at the provincial level. The episode established that "drastic measures" framing signals genuine community exhaustion but consistently produces displacement rather than resolution, and that the most powerful thing local politicians and residents can do is replace vague enforcement calls with specific, documented, time-bound provincial demands backed by electoral accountability.

## Deep Dive: Cariboo Connections - Cariboo Local Affairs
**Central Question:** Can municipal enforcement tools ('drastic measures') effectively address prolific offending in small cities, or do solutions require provincial-level intervention?

**Riley's Take:** Municipalities cannot solve this alone, but they are not powerless — they should act as precise translators of documented, specific needs to the province, and existing infrastructure like Foundry Cariboo Chilcotin proves that integrated service models can be built in this region.

**Casey's Take:** Enforcement measures at the municipal level move people around without resolving anything, and Williams Lake lacks the decade-long political commitment and provincial funding alignment that made comparable models like Medicine Hat's work — precision in asks is better than vagueness, but precision alone does not close the gap when the province has a track record of listening politely and funding slowly.

**Where They Converge:** Riley and Casey agreed that specificity and public accountability matter more than the framing of "drastic measures." Concretely, they agreed that Williams Lake council should: (1) document the actual number of individuals in the "prolific" category along with their full system interaction history; (2) make explicit, named provincial asks — specifically a dedicated adult Assertive Community Treatment (ACT) team — with attached timelines; (3) conduct a bylaw review that distinguishes what the city can genuinely regulate from what it is offloading onto police; and (4) create a public record of what was requested and what the province responded, so that electoral accountability becomes possible if the province fails to act.

## Recommendations & Action Items

- **Attend or watch Williams Lake City Council meetings** and ask, on the record, whether councillors have submitted a formal written request to the BC Ministry of Mental Health and Addictions for a dedicated adult Assertive Community Treatment (ACT) team in the Cariboo region — and if so, what the province's written response was and what the follow-up timeline is.
- **Request a public data briefing** from Williams Lake city staff or the RCMP Williams Lake Detachment on the actual number of individuals classified as prolific offenders, how many system interactions they have collectively had, and what interventions have been attempted — "prolific offenders" as a category may mean eight people or fifty, and the appropriate response differs entirely.
- **Contact your MLA for Cariboo-Chilcotin or Cariboo North** and ask specifically whether the province will fund an adult ACT team for the Williams Lake area, request a written answer with a timeline, and keep a copy — this creates the documentary record that makes non-response visible at election time.
- **Ask Foundry Cariboo Chilcotin directly** whether their integrated youth services model has been formally proposed to the province as a template for an adult-facing service, and whether they have capacity or interest in expanding to adult populations with provincial support. Their website is foundrybc.ca.
- **If you are a business owner or resident in downtown Williams Lake**, document specific incidents with dates, locations, and outcomes (police called, bylaw contacted, result) and submit that documentation to city council in writing — not at an open mic, but as a formal submission. Anecdote becomes evidence when it is aggregated and dated.
- **Follow the provincial local government consultations** currently scheduled for Cariboo communities and attend if possible — bring the ACT team ask and the bylaw review question as specific agenda items rather than general concerns, and ask what the formal mechanism is for tracking whether consultation input leads to a budget or policy change.
- **Monitor Cariboo-Prince George MP Todd Doherty's activities** around rural service infrastructure — given the Pacific Coastal Airlines loss and concurrent healthcare pressures, there may be a federal angle to rural mental health and addictions service gaps worth raising with his office directly.
- **If you are a voter in Williams Lake**, treat the next municipal election as an opportunity to ask every candidate whether they will commit to a public accountability log — a published record of every formal provincial ask made by council, the date it was made, and the response received — on issues including prolific offending, mental health services, and healthcare capacity.
