# Cariboo Signals — Corrections Policy

*Effective May 2026*

---

## How to Submit a Correction

If you believe an episode contains a factual error, misattribution, or misleading framing, please contact us:

- **Email:** feedback@cariboosignals.ca — subject line "Correction: [episode date or title]" (preferred; emails that mention a correction in the subject or opening lines are also detected)
- **GitHub Issue:** [github.com/zirnhelt/curated-podcast-generator/issues](https://github.com/zirnhelt/curated-podcast-generator/issues) — use the label "correction"

Please include: the episode date, the specific claim you believe is incorrect, and what you believe the correct information to be. Sources are helpful but not required.

---

## How Corrections Are Handled

1. **Acknowledgement** — We will acknowledge receipt within 3 business days.
2. **Review** — We will assess the correction against available sources.
3. **If the correction is valid** — We will note it at the end of the next episode's News Roundup — before the Community Spotlight segment is mentioned — stating clearly which episode the original error aired in and what the correct information is, and, where possible, update any written transcript or citations file associated with the original episode.
4. **If we disagree** — We will respond with our reasoning. If you believe we've got it wrong, we welcome further discussion.

---

## What We Correct

- Factual errors (incorrect statistics, misidentified organizations, wrong dates)
- Misattributions (claims attributed to the wrong person or source)
- Significant omissions that materially change the meaning of a segment

We do not issue corrections for differences of interpretation, opinion, or framing where reasonable people disagree.

---

## Archive

Corrections issued are noted in the episode transcript and, where applicable, in the relevant `citations.json` file. A running log is maintained in [GitHub Issues](https://github.com/zirnhelt/curated-podcast-generator/issues) under the "correction" label.
