Casey: Who enforces that in an unincorporated area, though? 100 Mile House can pass a bylaw covering its own municipal streetlights. It has zero authority over the highway lighting BC Hydro maintains, or a rural property owner's yard floodlight that's been blazing all night for twenty years.

Municipal lighting retrofits are usually revenue-neutral within a handful of years — shielded LED fixtures draw less power and need fewer replacements than the older high-pressure sodium lamps most small BC towns are still running.
