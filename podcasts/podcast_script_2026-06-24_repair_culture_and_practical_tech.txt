**DEEP DIVE: CARIBOO CONNECTIONS — Repair Culture & Practical Tech**

**RILEY:** The question worth sitting with on repair culture is this: when does the decision to repair something — rather than replace it — stop being a personal choice and start being a structural necessity? In the Cariboo, that line is drawn differently than it is in Vancouver or Calgary. You're not choosing the repair ethos as a lifestyle. In a lot of cases, you're choosing it because the alternative is waiting two weeks for a part or driving four hours to a service centre.

**CASEY:** And the repair economy in rural BC isn't a subculture. It's infrastructure. The person who can fix the walk-in cooler at a remote lodge, rebuild the hydraulic pump on a backhoe, or get a satellite receiver working again — that's not a hobbyist. That's the person keeping the operation alive.

**RILEY:** Right-to-repair legislation has been moving, slowly, in Canada. The federal government has acknowledged the issue. BC hasn't passed anything comprehensive yet, but the general direction — requiring manufacturers to provide service documentation, spare parts, and diagnostic access — is the right structural move. The EU's right-to-repair directive, which came into force in 2024, covers a growing list of product categories including phones, laptops, and home appliances. The practical effect is that manufacturers selling into Europe have to make repair more feasible, and that tends to ripple into North American product lines.

**CASEY:** Manufacturers comply with the letter of those requirements in ways that make repair technically possible but practically miserable. You can get the part. It costs 80% of the price of a new unit. The diagnostic software requires a dealer license. Apple did this with the Self Service Repair program — they launched it, got the press release, and then made the process complicated enough that most people still go to an authorized repair shop.

**RILEY:** Apple's Self Service Repair program is a real example of that gap. But iFixit's repairability scores have actually been improving on some Android flagship devices.
